47 unread quotes.

That's what was sitting in our G&G inbox when we pulled the data.

Sent. Never followed up on. Left to go cold while we were busy running jobs.

We did what every contractor does — quoted, moved on, assumed "if they want us they'll call back."

They didn't call back.

Here's what the data actually said:

The contractor who responds first wins 78% of the time.
Leads contacted within 5 minutes convert at 21x the rate of leads followed up an hour later.
80% of jobs close after 5+ touchpoints. We were averaging 1.

So we installed a Follow-Up Machine.

Day 2 after the quote: automatic check-in.
Day 5: value-add — "here's what to ask any contractor before you sign."
Day 10: soft urgency — "our schedule is filling up for that window."
Day 21: re-engagement — "did you go with someone? No hard feelings if so."
Day 30: close the loop.

We didn't change our pricing.
We didn't get better at sales.
We stopped forgetting.

Month 1: $26,000 recovered from cold estimates we'd written off.
Month 2: close rate on quoted jobs up 34%.

The money was already there.
It was sitting in quotes we'd abandoned.

If you run a trades business and you don't have an automated follow-up sequence running on every quote you send — you're leaving a significant portion of your revenue in the draft folder.

Not a pitch. Just math.
