---

Here's what was happening at Gables & Grove before we fixed this:

Estimate goes out. No response. We get busy. Three weeks pass. The job goes to someone else — not because they were cheaper, not because their work was better. Because they followed up and we didn't.

That's not a sales problem. That's a process problem.

The average contractor sends a quote and follows up once. Maybe twice. Then they move on. Meanwhile, that lead is still alive. Still deciding. Still waiting for someone to show up.

We weren't showing up.

---

So we built a follow-up sequence. Day 1. Day 3. Day 7. Day 14. Each message gets a little more direct. Each one offers something useful — an answer to a question they probably have, a look at a completed job like theirs, a reminder of what happens when they delay.

No spam. No pressure. Just the right message at the right time, sent automatically, while we're on the job site.

In 30 days: recovered 4 cold estimates. $26,000 in jobs we had already written off.

Close rate on quoted jobs up 34%.

We didn't hire a salesperson. We built a machine.

---

Count your last 30 days.

How many quotes did you send? How many did you follow up on more than once? How many jobs did you lose to silence — not because they said no, but because you went quiet first?

That gap is your number.
