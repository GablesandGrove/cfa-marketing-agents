$60,000. That's what we cut from our payroll when we stopped hiring admin and started building agents.

Not contractors. Not estimators. Admin. The people answering phones, chasing invoices, booking jobs, sending confirmations. The roles every contractor assumes they just have to pay for.

You don't.

---

Here's the math that changed how we run Gables & Grove:

**The hire:**
- Admin salary: $42,000/year
- Payroll taxes + benefits: ~$9,000/year
- 6–8 weeks to hire and train
- Gone in 14 months (industry average turnover)
- Start over: another $5,000–$8,000 in recruiting costs
- **Total cost, Year 1: ~$55,000–$60,000. For a role that is already leaving.**

**The build:**
- AI stack (all 6 agents): $10,000–$15,000 one-time
- Monthly retainer to maintain and improve: $1,500–$3,000
- Time to deploy: 3 days
- Turnover rate: zero
- Availability: 24/7
- **Year 1 cost: $25,000–$30,000. For a role that doesn't quit.**

We ran this at Gables & Grove. Replaced 2 admin roles. Cut $60K in overhead. The stack handles email triage, quote follow-ups, job scheduling, and onboarding. It was running in 3 days.

Year two, the gap gets wider. Because the AI doesn't ask for a raise.

---

The objection I hear most: "But AI can't talk to my customers."

Wrong framing. Your admin wasn't having conversations — they were sending confirmations, following up on quotes, routing calls, updating calendars. That's not relationship management. That's task execution. And task execution is exactly what AI does better than humans.

The real relationship is you. On the job. Delivering the work. That's irreplaceable.

Everything around it? Build it.
