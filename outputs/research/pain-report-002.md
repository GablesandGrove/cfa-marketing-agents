**Frequency:** 🔴 High

**Urgency:** Escalating. Lead market cost spike makes every missed follow-up more expensive than it was in 2024.

### Exact Language From Contractors

- *"When a customer requests a quote, you send it, they don't respond, and you move on — the job goes to a competitor"*

- *"I have quotes from 3 weeks ago I never followed up on"*

### Fresh Data (2026)

- First contractor to respond wins **78% of the time** (Hook Agency, 2026)
- Leads contacted within **5 minutes** are **21x** more likely to convert than those followed up with after 30 minutes

- The paid lead market (Angi, Thumbtack, HomeAdvisor) is collapsing: same lead now goes to **6–8 contractors** vs. 3–4 two years ago, at double the price
- G&G internal: recovered **$26K in 30 days** from cold estimates via automated follow-up sequences
- G&G internal: close rate on quoted jobs **up 34%** after 30-day automated follow-up

**Scaling Sam** — has volume, knows the problem, hasn't built the fix

### CFA Response

## Theme C: AI Without a Strategy — "We're Winging It and Burning Budget"

**Emotional tone:** Confused, mildly embarrassed, quietly worried
**Urgency:** High. The 78% of contractors "using" AI are mostly using it wrong — and competitors who are doing it right are pulling ahead.

### Exact Language From Contractors

- *"AI is just ChatGPT, right? I tried it, it didn't know my industry"*
- *"We have AI tools but nobody uses them consistently"*
- *"Rushing ends up with tool sprawl, burned budgets, and skeptical teams"* (HBR, Feb 2026)
- *"Most small businesses are experimenting with AI but very few are doing it strategically"* (Digital Applied, 2026)
- *"Many contractors assume adopting AI requires a complete systems overhaul"*
- *"I don't know where to start — and I can't afford to guess"*
- *"My team won't use it"*
- *"I bought tools. They collected dust."*

### Fresh Data (2026)

- **78% of contractors** are using or testing AI tools (BuildOps / ServiceTitan 2026 State of AI)
- But **most "wing it"** — no strategy, no training, no accountability (Digital Applied, 2026)
- "Rushing AI adoption ends up with **tool sprawl, burned budgets, and skeptical teams**" — HBR, Feb 2026
