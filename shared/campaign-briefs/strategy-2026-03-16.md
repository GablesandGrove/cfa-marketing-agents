## Brief A: Follow-Up Failure

### Strategic Position
The money is already in your quote folder. You just stopped chasing it.

### CFA Narrative
Every trades contractor we talk to has the same problem: they quote 30 jobs a month and follow up on 4. Not because they don't care — because there's no system and they're too busy surviving the day. We built the Follow-Up Machine inside our own painting company. Automated sequences. Day 2, 5, 10, 21, 30. Close rate on quoted jobs went up 34% in 60 days. We recovered $26K in one month from cold estimates that had been sitting dead. We install this for other contractors now.

### Hook Bank
1. `"47 leads last month. You followed up on 3. The other 44 hired someone else."` — Pain mirror
2. `"The contractor who responds first wins 78% of the time. You're still responding on Tuesday to Friday's lead."` — Urgency/data
3. `"We recovered $26K in 30 days. We didn't get new leads. We followed up on old ones."` — Proof teaser
4. `"Your competitors hired a salesperson. We installed a machine that never sleeps."` — Contrarian
5. `"80% of contractor jobs close after 5+ follow-ups. Most contractors quit after 1. Do the math."` — Data-driven shame
6. `"The quote you sent and forgot about? It's still sitting in their inbox."` — Pain stinger
7. `"Speed-to-lead is dead simple: first one in wins. You're not first. Here's why."` — Direct challenge
8. `"We didn't get better at sales. We stopped forgetting."` — Credibility/mechanism
9. `"Angi just doubled the price of your leads. Meanwhile you're not following up on the ones you already have."` — Urgency tie-in
10. `"Your follow-up system is your memory. Right now, your memory is full."` — Analogy

### Channel Angles

- **LinkedIn:** Operator essay, 300–400 words. Lead with the $26K recovery story. Show the before state (Friday lead, Tuesday follow-up, already hired). Reveal the mechanism. End with soft CTA. **Format:** Story post, no list, no bullet points — straight prose like a text from a peer. CCN: Casual.
- **Instagram:** Reels. 20–30 sec. Open with split-stat text overlay: "Contractor A followed up in 5 minutes. Contractor B followed up Tuesday. Contractor A got the job. 78% of the time, this is what happens." Cut to: "We installed the fix." Hard CTA. CCN: New.
- **YouTube Short:** 45–55 sec. "We pulled 6 months of follow-up data from our painting company. Here's what the numbers said." Screen recording with voiceover. Show the before (0 sequences, manual tracking) vs. after (automated sequences, 34% close rate lift). CCN: Core.
- **Blog:** SEO. "Why the First Contractor to Respond Wins (And How to Build a System That Makes You First Every Time)." 1,200 words. Proof-led. Includes G&G data, methodology, CTA to Assessment. CCN: Casual/New.

### CTA Direction
- **Core:** "DM me FOLLOWUP — I'll show you what the sequence looks like inside G&G."
- **Casual:** "Save this. Then count how many open quotes you have right now."
- **New:** No CTA. End on the stat. Let it land.

### Archetype Target
- **Primary:** Grind-Mode Gary (no system, quotes die in his inbox)
- **Secondary:** Scaling Sam (has volume, knows the problem, needs the push to act)

### Content Intent
Proof-driven (primary). Aggressive (secondary — the Angi lead cost angle).

### Top 3 Hooks for Content Writer
1. `"47 leads last month. You followed up on 3. The other 44 hired someone else."`
2. `"We recovered $26K in 30 days. Not from new leads. From old ones we stopped ignoring."`

## Brief B: Software Overload

### Strategic Position
You don't have a software problem. You have an integration problem. And adding another app won't fix it.

### CFA Narrative
Contractors are paying $400–$800/month across Jobber, QuickBooks, a CRM, a scheduling tool, and a proposal builder — and none of them talk to each other. When a lead comes in, someone has to manually move data between 4 systems. That someone is Fred. Or a $45K/year office manager who also can't keep up. We built one stack where the agents share a single source of truth. Lead comes in — it's logged, contextualized, and in motion before Fred looks up from the job site.

### Hook Bank
1. `"Your CRM is obsolete."` — Pure aggression, contrarian
2. `"You're paying $600/month for 6 apps. None of them talk to each other. We fixed this."` — Proof/credibility
3. `"Every time a lead comes in, someone at your company copies and pastes. Stop that."` — Pain mirror
4. `"ServiceTitan is for billion-dollar companies. Jobber is for people who outgrow Jobber. Neither was built for you."` — Market positioning
5. `"6 logins. 6 tabs. 6 monthly fees. Still not having everything in one place."` — Pain stinger (direct contractor language)
6. `"You're the integration layer in your own business. Every time you switch apps, that's on you."` — Framing hook
7. `"Your stack should work while you're on a ladder. Does it?"` — Contrarian challenge
8. `"We killed the copy-paste. Here's what replaced it."` — Curiosity/mechanism
9. `"The problem isn't the software. It's that no one built it for contractors."` — Positioning hook
10. `"2025: you buy tools. 2026: the tools work together. Welcome to the stack."` — Urgency/era framing

### Channel Angles

- **LinkedIn:** "The software graveyard." Operator POV post listing the tools Fred pays for, what each costs, and what each still can't do. No jargon. End with: "We don't sell another app. We build the layer that makes all of them irrelevant." CCN: Casual. **Format:** Short numbered list post with a punchy close.
- **Instagram:** Split graphic reel — "Before CFA: 6 apps, 6 logins, copy-paste between all of them. After CFA: 1 stack, agents reading the same data, zero manual handoffs." Text-on-screen format, 15–20 sec. CCN: New.
- **YouTube Short:** Real-time screen recording. "I'm logging into every tool a typical painting contractor uses by 8am. Watch how long it takes just to check on one lead." Show the friction. Don't solve it in this video — let the problem speak. CCN: New/Casual.
- **Blog:** "Why Jobber and ServiceTitan Are Creating More Work for Small Contractors." SEO-targeted on "[software name] alternatives for contractors." Comparison format. CTA to Assessment. CCN: Casual/New.

### CTA Direction
- **Core:** "DM me STACK — I'll show you what one source of truth looks like for a 10-person contractor."
- **Casual:** "Follow to see how the stack works. We're building it in public."
- **New:** No CTA. Let the pain land. End on a statement: "You shouldn't be the integration layer in your own business."

### Archetype Target
- **Primary:** Scaling Sam (has tools, hates how they work, ready for the real solution)
- **Secondary:** Multi-Trade Mike (running multiple lines with zero unified view)

### Content Intent
Aggressive (primary). Educational (secondary — show the mechanism once credibility is established).

### Top 3 Hooks for Content Writer
1. `"Your CRM is obsolete."`
2. `"6 logins. 6 tabs. 6 monthly fees. Still missing things."`
3. `"You're the integration layer in your own business. Every time you switch apps, that's on you."`

## Brief C: AI Without a Strategy

### Strategic Position
78% of contractors are "using AI." Most of them are burning budget and calling it progress.

### CFA Narrative
Every contractor has tried ChatGPT. Most used it once, got a generic response, and filed AI under "not for my business." The ones who stuck with it bought tools that their team never opened. 18 months of development, 5 developers, and one real $2M painting company as the test environment — that's how you build an AI stack that actually works for trades. We burned the budget so Fred doesn't have to. The retainer isn't maintenance — it's ongoing R&D. We stay current. You get the results.

### Hook Bank
1. `"78% of contractors are using AI. Most of them are winging it."` — Data-driven, contrarian
2. `"ChatGPT doesn't know what a VOC primer is. Our agents do."` — Specificity/credibility
3. `"You bought AI tools. Your team didn't use them. $400/month. Dust."` — Pain mirror
4. `"Contractors who figure out AI in 2026 will eat the ones who don't."` — Power phrase / urgency
5. `"We spent 18 months building what ChatGPT couldn't do for contractors."` — Credibility drop

7. `"You don't need more AI tools. You need the right stack, installed right, the first time."` — Positioning
8. `"One operator + AI > a team of five without it. We proved it."` — Power phrase with proof
9. `"The contractors who are winning right now aren't using AI more. They're using it smarter."` — Peer comparison
10. `"We stay current so you don't have to. That's the whole deal."` — Retainer positioning anchor

### Channel Angles

- **LinkedIn:** Credibility essay. "78% of contractors are using AI. Here's why most of them are still falling behind." Walk through the difference between tool-buying and stack-building.
