## Primary Target: Frustrated Fred (Master Profile)

Fred is not one person. Fred is every trades contractor running a real business who is drowning in the work of running a real business. He spans all trades. He is always the decision maker. He IS the business.

**Revenue:** $800K–$3M annually
**Trades:** Painting, roofing, flooring, HVAC, landscaping, GC, concrete, drywall — all trades
**Team:** 3–15 employees
**Years in business:** 5–12 years
**Decision maker:** 100%. No committee. No approval chain. Fred says yes, it happens.

---

## Fred's Day

```
6:00am  — Phone goes off. Three texts from customers, one from a supplier, two from crew.
          All need answers he hasn't had coffee yet to give.

7:30am  — Driving to the job site. On the phone the whole way.
          The foreman question could've been handled if there was a system for it.

10:00am — Customer asks why the estimate hasn't arrived yet.
          Fred's notes are on a napkin somewhere in the truck.

1:00pm  — 47 unread emails. He marks all as read.
          That's not managing email. That's surviving email.

6:30pm  — Gets home. Opens the laptop to write estimates.
          Falls asleep at the desk at 9pm.

Repeat.
```

---

## Fred's Fears

These are real. Don't minimize them. Address them head-on.

- **"If I'm not there, nothing gets done right"** — He's been proven correct too many times.
- **"Scaling means MORE hours, not less"** — Every hire has added stress, not reduced it.
- **"I hire someone and they screw it up"** — His name is on every job.
- **"Technology will make me obsolete"** — He doesn't understand AI yet, and that's terrifying.
- **"I'll burn out before I enjoy what I built"** — The business is winning. Fred is losing.

---

## Fred's Dreams

These are what he won't say out loud but thinks about every Sunday night.

- **A business that runs without him as the bottleneck** — Not sold, not closed — just not needed for every decision.
- **Take a real vacation with his phone off** — He hasn't done it in years. Maybe ever.
- **Look like a big operation with a lean team** — Beat the bigger companies without their overhead.
- **Be the guy other contractors ask about** — He follows those guys now. He wants to be one.
- **Financial freedom — actually keeping more of it** — Revenue is up. Take-home hasn't moved.

---

## What Makes Fred Buy

1. Proof from someone who runs what he runs — not theory, not software pitches
2. A specific number that sounds like his business ("47 unread emails" — he felt that)
3. Someone who speaks trades, not tech
4. A low-risk entry point — he's been burned before
5. Clear ROI — not "efficiency gains," actual dollars or hours

---

## What Makes Fred Bounce

1. Sounds like a consultant
2. Vague claims ("streamline your workflow")
3. No proof of trades experience
4. Feature lists instead of outcome promises
5. Anything longer than 90 seconds on video or 150 words in a post

## Sub-Archetypes

Tag every content piece to one of these. They are all variations of Fred at different stages and orientations.

---

### Grind-Mode Gary
> *"I built it with my hands. Now I can't get out of it."*

**Stage:** Fred at an earlier, rawer stage
**Revenue:** $300K–$1.5M
**Trade:** Painting, landscaping, cleaning, flooring — field-heavy work
**Situation:** He's the crew lead AND the owner. Can't step back without the job falling apart.
**Core pain:** Time. Every minute he's on the tools is a minute he's not running the business.
**What he needs first:** Basic systems. Document, delegate, then automate.
**CFA entry:** Starter Stack. Low-barrier Assessment.
**Content tone:** Raw. Empathetic. Short. Hook on his day, not the solution.
**Best channels:** Instagram, TikTok, Facebook Groups
**Power hook:** "You can't grow a company from a ladder."

---

### Scaling Sam
> *"I know I need AI. I don't know where to start — and I can't afford to guess."*

**Stage:** Fred who has some infrastructure and is ready to scale deliberately
**Revenue:** $1M–$5M
**Trade:** All trades — typically HVAC, roofing, painting, plumbing at this level
**Situation:** Has an office manager or ops person. Knows the business can't keep scaling the old way. Has been hearing "AI" for 18 months and hasn't acted yet.
**Core pain:** Clarity + confidence. He'll spend money. He just needs to know it's the right bet.
**What he needs:** Full Stack or Retainer. He is the high-value buyer.
**CFA entry:** Assessment → Full Stack → Retainer
**Content tone:** Direct. Operator POV. Credibility-first. Show the ROI.
**Best channels:** LinkedIn, Podcast ads, Direct outreach
**Power hook:** "One operator + AI > team of five without it."

---

### Aspiring Alex
> *"I'm just getting started. I want to build it right from day one."*

**Stage:** Pre-Fred. Building the business now.
**Revenue:** Under $500K
**Trade:** Often painting, landscaping, handyman, cleaning — low barrier to entry trades
**Situation:** Solo or tiny team. Figured out how to get work. Now needs to figure out how to systematize before it breaks him.
**Core pain:** Doesn't know what he doesn't know. Building blind.
**What he needs:** A playbook. Not yet a full CFA client — future Tikrr user.
**CFA role:** Nurture now. Convert later. Treat like a VIP.
**Content tone:** Educational. Generous. "Here's what we wish we knew."
**Best channels:** TikTok, YouTube, Instagram
**Power hook:** "Build the system before the system breaks you."
**Note:** Do NOT pitch Alex hard. Give him massive value. He'll be Sam in 3 years.

---

### Multi-Trade Mike
> *"I run three service lines and none of them talk to each other."*

**Stage:** Experienced operator, complex business
**Revenue:** $2M–$8M across multiple trades or divisions
**Situation:** Has a roofing crew, a painting crew, maybe a maintenance arm. Everything is siloed. He's the only person who sees the whole picture.
**Core pain:** Coordination chaos. Nothing integrates. He's the integration layer.
**What he needs:** Unified stack. Single source of truth across divisions.
**CFA entry:** Full Stack with custom integrations. Premium buyer.
**Content tone:** Systems-focused. Complexity acknowledged but solved.
**Best channels:** LinkedIn, Direct outreach, Referral
**Power hook:** "You shouldn't be the only person who knows what's happening in your own company."

---

### Modern Ops Olivia
> *"We already use tech. I want the next level."*

**Stage:** Tech-forward operator who's ahead of the curve
**Revenue:** $1M–$6M
**Trade:** Often painting, HVAC, or specialty contractors who moved early on software
**Situation:** Uses a CRM, has a decent ops setup, isn't drowning. But knows there's a ceiling and AI is the ceiling-breaker.
**Core pain:** Not pain — opportunity. She wants the edge before it becomes standard.
**What she needs:** Advanced stack. Integrations. Retainer to stay current.
**CFA entry:** Assessment → Direct to Retainer
**Content tone:** Peer-to-peer. She's smart. Don't over-explain. Show the frontier.
**Best channels:** LinkedIn, Email, Podcast
**Power hook:** "Your current stack is good. Here's what makes it great."

---

### Edge-Seeking Ethan
> *"I want to be first in my market. What's actually next?"*

**Stage:** Early adopter. Technologist-operator hybrid.
**Revenue:** $500K–$3M
**Trade:** Any — often runs a differentiated operation already
**Situation:** Follows AI developments. Has experimented with tools. Wants someone who can keep him ahead, not catch him up.
**Core pain:** Finding a partner who is actually ahead of him, not selling him yesterday's tools.
**What he needs:** Retainer. Access to the frontier. Proof that CFA is ahead.
**CFA entry:** Retainer-first conversation. Lead with what's new.
**Content tone:** Forward-looking. Bold claims with proof. "Here's what's working right now."
**Best channels:** Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Newsletter, Direct
**Power hook:** "Contractors who figure out AI in 2026 will eat the ones who don't."

## CCN Buyer Modes

Every piece of content maps to one of three buyer modes. Know which one you're writing for before you write a word.

### CORE — Ready to Buy
**Who:** Active buyers. Already following CFA. Have the pain, have the budget, need the push.
**Goal:** Convert to a call or DM.
**Tone:** Direct. Urgency. Clear offer.
**CTA style:** Hard CTA — "Book a call." "DM me 'STACK'." "Link in bio."
**Content examples:** Assessment offer posts, case studies, before/after proof, direct offer

---

### CASUAL — Warming Up
**Who:** Aware of CFA. Watching. Not ready yet. Building trust.
**Goal:** Stay top of mind. Build credibility. Make them save or share.
**Tone:** Credible and useful. No hard sell.
**CTA style:** Soft CTA — "Save this." "Share with a contractor who needs this." "Follow for more."
**Content examples:** Educational breakdowns, operator tips, "here's how we do it" content, objection handling

---

### NEW — Cold Traffic
**Who:** Never seen CFA. First impression. Skeptical by default.
**Goal:** Stop the scroll. Create curiosity. Do NOT sell.
**Tone:** Pure value. Zero jargon. Hook on a universal contractor pain.
**CTA style:** None, or passive — "Follow if this hit."
**Content examples:** Day-in-the-life hooks, relatable pain posts, contrarian takes, viral-format content

---

**Rule:** One piece of content, one buyer mode. Don't try to convert AND educate AND entertain in the same post. Pick the mode, write for it, test it.
